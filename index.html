Pioneering Women in Science: Spotlight on Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a pioneering physicist and chemist who became the first woman to win a Nobel Prize—and the only person to ever win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields: Physics and Chemistry.

Full Name: Maria Sklodowska-Curie
Born: November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland
Died: July 4, 1934, in France
Fields: Physics, Chemistry
Notable Achievements:
Discovered the radioactive elements polonium and radium
Coined the term radioactivity
Won the 1903 Nobel Prize in Physics (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel)
Won the 1911 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her work on radium and polonium

Other Trailblazing Women in Science

Asima Chatterjee

Field: Organic Chemistry Known for: Pioneering work in developing drugs for epilepsy and malaria. Legacy: First woman to earn a Doctorate of Science from an Indian university.

Ada Lovelace

Known as the first computer programmer, Ada Lovelace developed algorithms that would lay the foundation for modern computing.

Rosalind Franklin

Her x-ray images contributed to the discovery of DNA's double helix structure, a pivotal moment in genetics.

Darleane Hoffman

Discovered element Seaborgium; expert in nuclear chemistry (Indian-American).

Emmy Noether

Emmy Noether (1882–1935) was a groundbreaking German mathematician whose work in abstract algebra and theoretical physics profoundly shaped modern science.

Bibha Chowdhur

First Indian woman physicist to work on cosmic rays; worked at TIFR.Worked at Bose Institute under Debendra Mohan Bose, focusing on cosmic ray particles.
